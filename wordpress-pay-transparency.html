A salary range is only as defensible as the job description underneath it.

Most state pay transparency laws require employers to post a range they "genuinely expect to pay." That expectation has to come from somewhere, and it should come from accurate, up-to-date job descriptions tied to market data. Without clean job architecture, salary ranges become guesswork and are difficult to defend to auditors or candidates.
