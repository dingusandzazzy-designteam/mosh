The 2,490-vs-100 question, answered

Your website is healthy. The scary number was the newsletter, not a bug.

Semrush was counting 2,490+ URLs because its audit was set to crawl the whole domain — including the beehiiv newsletter subdomain. The website itself has ~200 real pages, the sitemap is clean, and Google indexes exactly what it should. We reconfigured the audit; the report now reflects reality.

No backend bug. No ghost pages. Semrush's own data confirms Google won't index the excess: only 204 URLs are unique real pages — the other 2,412 are duplicates that point their canonical tag back to the real page. The gap between Semrush (2,490) and Google is crawler scope, not an indexability problem.
Confirmed by Google Search Console. Filtered to the submitted sitemap, Google reports 126 pages indexed + 73 discovered-but-not-yet-indexed = 199 — an exact match to the real site. The 73 aren't errors: 48 are simply queued (crawl priority) and 25 are thin/low-value pages. Nothing near 2,490. The sitemap is registered and actively used by Google.

Why the next part is cheap and high-leverage

Nearly every remaining issue is template-level, not per-page.

The site runs on WordPress + Elementor + All in One SEO. A handful of fixes — in the post template, the /freedownloads/ template, the schema block, and the footer — cascade across 20 to 190 pages each. That means a fast, visible win instead of a page-by-page slog.

The roadmap

How we take Health from 84% to 92%+ — and then grow traffic

1

Technical quick wins

~1 week · template-level · Health 84% → ~92%+, errors 22 → ~2

  • Fix the structured-data schema — the SoftwareApplication block emits empty rating/review/offers fields clears 20 errors
  • Repair heading structure in the post template — skipped H1/heading levels ~80 pages
  • Differentiate H1 from title in the post template 62 pages
  • Add H1s to the /freedownloads/ & legal templates 21 pages
  • Trim long titles to ≤60 chars & fix the footer HTTP link 32 pages
2

Index & sitemap hygiene

Keep only pages that should rank in the index

  • Drop sitemap.rss from robots.txt & fix the one non-canonical URL in the sitemap
  • noindex + de-list low-value/duplicate pages (old-faqs, phone-popup, thank-you, roi-calculator, dupe management page)
  • Set app.moshjd.com to noindex (dev-agency change)
3

On-page SEO & growth

The revenue lever — where rankings and traffic actually move

  • Internal linking pass — 34 pages have only one incoming link; route authority to money pages & new posts
  • Keyword-rich anchors — replace "Learn More / Read More / click here" (54 links)
  • Optimize the commercial posts first — Mosh JD vs. JDxpert / SHRM / Payscale, pricing, ROI
  • Topical clusters across the 131 blog posts — compounding authority in the "job description" niche
4

Ongoing monitoring

Keep the gains and catch regressions early

  • Monthly Semrush audit, scope locked to www.moshjd.com
  • Position tracking on target keywords + a content calendar
  • Quarterly technical re-check
